Blueberry Cheesecake

Ingredients
  • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 5 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 2 (8-ounce/227g blocks) cream cheese
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large eggs
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 cup blueberries
  • 2 cups blueberries
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tbsp water
  • 2 tsp cornstarch
Instructions
  1. Lightly spray the inside of a 9-inch springform pan with baking spray.
  2. In a medium bowl, stir together the graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and sugar. Press into the bottom and about 2-inches up sides of the prepared pan. Refrigerate while preparing the filling, or at least 30 minutes.
  3. Preheat the oven to 325Â°F. Position one oven rack in the lowest position and the other oven rack in the middle of the oven.
  4. In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the cream cheese and sugar on medium-high speed until smooth and fluffy, about 2 to 3 minutes. Add the sour cream and beat on medium speed until combined, about 1 minute. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stop and scrape down the bowl a few times during mixing. Mix in the flour and vanilla until smooth, about 1 minute.
  5. Gently fold in the blueberries. Pour the batter into the chilled crust, spreading the top into an even layer.
  6. Place a large ovenproof casserole dish on the bottom rack of the oven. Fill the dish with boiling water. Place the cheesecake on the rack above the dish.
  7. Bake for 1 hour and 15 minutes or until the filling is set, begins to pull away from sides and the center is wobbly when gently shaken. Tent with foil if the top starts to brown during baking. Let cool on a wire rack, for 1 hour. Refrigerate, uncovered, for at least 4 and up to 24 hours.
  8. In a small saucepan, bring 1 1/2 cups of blueberries, sugar, and lemon juice to a simmer over medium heat. Cook, stirring often, until the blueberries breakdown and the sauce is syrupy, about 8 minutes.
  9. In a small bowl, whisk together the water and cornstarch. Stir it into the pan along with the remaining 1/2 cup blueberries. Cook, stirring constantly until thickened, about 2 minutes. Remove from heat, and let cool to room temperature, about 30 minutes.
  10. Spoon over the cooled cheesecake before slicing.
